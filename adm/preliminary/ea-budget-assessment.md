# Overview

This document summarizes the current and planned budget requirements supporting Enterprise Architecture capability within the Celejewski Family Enterprise ecosystem.

The enterprise operates with:

* lightweight financial governance,
* pragmatic investment decisions,
* incremental capability development,
* controlled operational spending.

Budget-related decisions are currently evaluated jointly at the household level and are treated as part of broader long-term investment in:

* professional development,
* architecture capability,
* cloud capability,
* software engineering capability,
* operational sustainability.

# Financial Governance Characteristics

Budget decisions related to Enterprise Architecture activities currently remain:

* lightweight,
* household-coordinated,
* incrementally evaluated,
* sustainability-oriented.

Architecture-related spending is typically discussed jointly at the household level, especially for:

* certification expenses,
* AI tooling subscriptions,
* operational cloud spending,
* long-term learning investments.

The enterprise intentionally avoids:

* excessive operational spending,
* unnecessary tooling complexity,
* high-maintenance infrastructure investments.

# Budget Constraints

The enterprise currently operates within important financial and operational constraints including:

* limited household financial capacity,
* balancing learning investments with household priorities,
* balancing experimentation with sustainability,
* limited time and cognitive bandwidth.

As a result:

* investments are evaluated pragmatically,
* operational simplicity is strongly preferred,
* low-overhead tooling is favored,
* experimentation remains incremental and controlled.

# Overall Assessment

The current Enterprise Architecture budget model is considered:

The enterprise demonstrates meaningful willingness to invest in:

Current spending levels remain relatively low while still enabling:

* real operational experience,
