# Architecture Principles Assessment

Project: Celejewski Family Enterprise
ADM Phase: Preliminary Phase
Step: Identify and Establish Architecture Principles
Status: Draft
Last Updated: 2026-06-10
Owner: Sebastian Celejewski

# Overview

This document summarizes the identification, assessment, and establishment of Architecture Principles for the Celejewski Family Enterprise ecosystem.

The principles are documented in:

* `/architecture/principles.md`

The purpose of this assessment is to:

* confirm the existence of explicit architecture principles,
* verify alignment between principles and enterprise characteristics,
* confirm governance applicability,
* validate consistency with enterprise goals, constraints, and operational realities.

The principles primarily guide architecture decisions within the Chores Cooperative (ChoCoop) ecosystem while also influencing broader enterprise architecture evolution across the family ecosystem.

# Architecture Principle Characteristics

The current Architecture Principles are intentionally:

* lightweight,
* pragmatic,
* operationally grounded,
* sustainability-oriented,
* experimentation-friendly,
* socio-technical rather than purely technical.

The principles strongly reflect:

* enterprise goals,
* operational constraints,
* governance philosophy,
* architecture maturity,
* stakeholder dynamics,
* cognitive and organizational limitations.

The principle model intentionally avoids:

* excessive enterprise bureaucracy,
* overly abstract governance language,
* technology dogmatism,
* unrealistic operational assumptions.

* enterprise scale,
* operational model,
* architecture maturity,
* governance philosophy,

The principles support:

* limited cognitive capacity,

* stakeholder diversity,

# Operational and Socio-Technical Alignment

The principles intentionally balance:

* technical concerns,
* operational concerns,
* stakeholder well-being,
* learning goals,
* governance sustainability.

Several principles explicitly acknowledge that:

* technology alone does not determine enterprise effectiveness,
* architecture decisions affect human behavior and emotional dynamics,
* governance must remain sustainable,
* architecture exists within real operational and cognitive constraints.

This socio-technical orientation is considered one of the defining characteristics of the current architecture approach.
