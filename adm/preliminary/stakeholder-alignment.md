# Overview

This document summarizes current stakeholder alignment, sponsorship characteristics, and organizational support related to Enterprise Architecture work within the Celejewski Family Enterprise ecosystem.

Because the enterprise operates as a small family-oriented socio-technical ecosystem:

* sponsorship structures remain informal,
* governance relationships are lightweight,
* stakeholder alignment is based primarily on ongoing participation, operational acceptance, and long-term support rather than formal approval processes.

The purpose of this assessment is to:

* identify current architecture sponsors and supporting stakeholders,
* confirm organizational acceptance of architecture activities,
* understand stakeholder alignment with enterprise evolution,
* identify factors supporting long-term sustainability of Enterprise Architecture capability.

# Enterprise Sponsorship Model

The enterprise currently operates with:

* highly centralized architecture ownership,
* lightweight governance,
* informal but meaningful sponsorship relationships.

Architecture activities are currently sponsored primarily through:

* operational acceptance,
* financial acceptance,
* long-term capability investment support,
* tolerance for experimentation and learning activities.

The enterprise does not currently maintain:

* formal architecture boards,
* formal governance committees,
* formal approval workflows,
* dedicated architecture funding structures.

These limitations are considered appropriate for the current enterprise scale.

* unrealistic organizational complexity.
