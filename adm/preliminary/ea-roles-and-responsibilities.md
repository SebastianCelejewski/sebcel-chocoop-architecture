# Overview

This document defines the currently existing Enterprise Architecture-related roles, responsibilities, and governance ownership within the Celejewski Family Enterprise ecosystem.

The enterprise operates as a small and highly centralized socio-technical ecosystem. As a result:

* multiple architecture and governance roles are currently performed by a single primary stakeholder,
* governance mechanisms remain intentionally lightweight,
* operational and architecture responsibilities are strongly integrated.

The purpose of this document is to:

* clarify architecture-related ownership,
* define governance responsibilities,
* identify stakeholder participation areas,
* support Enterprise Architecture capability formalization.

# Current Organizational Model

The current enterprise architecture operating model is:

* highly centralized,
* lightweight,
* operationally integrated,
* experimentation-oriented,
* repository-driven.

Architecture, governance, operational, and development activities are currently coordinated primarily by Sebastian Celejewski.

The enterprise intentionally favors:

* low coordination overhead,
* fast decision-making,
* incremental evolution,
* lightweight governance,
* operational flexibility.

# Governance Ownership Assessment

The current governance model is intentionally:

* centralized,
* lightweight,
* highly adaptive.

Advantages include:

* fast decision-making,
* low organizational overhead,
* rapid experimentation capability,
* operational consistency.

At the same time, the model creates:

* strong dependency on a single primary owner,
* limited governance redundancy,
* limited review diversity,
* partially implicit governance practices.

These limitations are currently considered acceptable given:

* enterprise size,
* operational scale,
* experimentation-oriented culture,
* lightweight governance philosophy.
