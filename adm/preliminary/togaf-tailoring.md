# Overview

This document describes how the TOGAF Standard and related architecture practices are tailored to the specific characteristics of the Celejewski Family Enterprise ecosystem.

The enterprise differs significantly from traditional large-scale organizations because it operates as:

* a small family-oriented socio-technical ecosystem,
* a lightweight operational environment,
* an experimentation-oriented architecture environment,
* a learning-driven ecosystem,
* a highly centralized governance model.

As a result, the architecture approach intentionally applies:

* selective TOGAF adoption,
* lightweight governance,
* pragmatic documentation,
* incremental architecture evolution,
* simplified organizational structures.

The purpose of tailoring is not to reduce architectural quality, but to:

* align architecture practices with enterprise scale,
* ensure operational sustainability,
* avoid unnecessary governance overhead,
* support realistic long-term maintainability.

# Tailoring Philosophy

The enterprise intentionally treats TOGAF as:

* a guidance framework,
* a thinking model,
* a governance vocabulary,
* a capability analysis framework,
  rather than:
* a rigid procedural methodology.

The enterprise explicitly avoids:

* ceremonial governance,
* unnecessary process complexity,
* heavyweight enterprise structures,
* architecture activities disconnected from operational reality.

Architecture practices are intentionally adapted to:

* available cognitive capacity,
* available operational capacity,
* enterprise scale,
* experimentation-oriented evolution,
* long-term sustainability.

# Governance Tailoring

## Lightweight Governance Model

Traditional TOGAF governance structures such as:

* Architecture Boards,
* governance committees,
* formal review councils,
* dedicated architecture offices

are currently replaced with:

* centralized architecture ownership,
* repository-driven governance,
* lightweight operational review,
* incremental architecture validation.

### Rationale

The enterprise scale does not justify heavyweight governance structures.

The current governance model intentionally prioritizes:

* low coordination overhead,
* fast decision-making,
* operational flexibility,
* sustainability.

## Lightweight Compliance Validation

Formal compliance assessment processes are intentionally simplified.

Current governance relies primarily on:

* architecture principles,
* ADR documentation,
* operational review,
* iterative architecture reflection.

### Rationale

Heavy compliance processes would create disproportionate operational overhead relative to enterprise scale.

# ADM Tailoring

## Incremental and Iterative ADM Execution

The enterprise does not execute ADM as a rigid sequential process.

Instead:

* ADM activities evolve iteratively,
* earlier artifacts are continuously refined,
* enterprise understanding evolves incrementally,
* architecture maturity develops progressively.

### Examples

Current ADM execution includes:

* repeated refinement of enterprise definitions,
* evolving capability understanding,
* iterative governance formalization,
* continuous architecture learning.

### Rationale

The enterprise intentionally favors:

* continuous evolution,
* iterative learning,
* incremental maturity growth,
  rather than:
* large upfront modeling exercises.

## Scope Tailoring

The current ADM effort focuses primarily on:

* Chores Cooperative (ChoCoop),
  while still recognizing:
* broader enterprise ecosystem context.

Other family systems are currently treated primarily as:

* contextual enterprise capabilities,
* emerging ecosystem elements,
  rather than:
* full architecture scope areas.

### Rationale

This allows:

* controlled scope,
* manageable architecture complexity,
* focused governance evolution.

# Repository and Documentation Tailoring

## Markdown-Centric Architecture Repository

Architecture artifacts are maintained primarily as:

* Markdown documents,
* repository-managed artifacts,
* version-controlled architecture knowledge.

The enterprise intentionally avoids:

* heavyweight architecture repository platforms,
* specialized enterprise tooling,
* large governance suites.

### Rationale

Repository-driven architecture supports:

* simplicity,
* traceability,
* maintainability,
* low operational overhead,
* integration with development workflows.

## Documentation as Incremental Knowledge Evolution

Architecture documentation is treated as:

* evolving knowledge,
* operational guidance,
* governance support,
* historical context.

Documentation is intentionally allowed to evolve incrementally together with:

* architecture understanding,
* operational maturity,
* governance capability.

### Rationale

The enterprise favors:

* continuous refinement,
  over:
* large static documentation efforts.

# Modeling Tailoring

## Lightweight Modeling Approach

The enterprise intentionally uses:

* lightweight textual modeling,
* conceptual capability analysis,
* practical operational descriptions,
* incremental architecture diagrams where useful.

The enterprise currently avoids:

* excessive formal notation,
* large-scale meta-modeling,
* heavy architecture abstraction.

### Rationale

The current ecosystem complexity does not justify heavy formal modeling overhead.

The enterprise prioritizes:

* understandability,
* maintainability,
* practical usefulness.

## Socio-Technical Modeling

Architecture analysis explicitly includes:

* emotional dynamics,
* motivation patterns,
* stakeholder interaction preferences,
* family communication patterns,
* behavioral observations.

### Rationale

The enterprise ecosystem is fundamentally socio-technical rather than purely technical.

Behavioral and emotional dynamics significantly influence enterprise effectiveness.

# Operational Tailoring

## DevOps-Oriented Operational Model

Architecture and operations are intentionally strongly integrated.

The enterprise uses:

* cloud-native operational practices,
* incremental deployments,
* rollback-oriented thinking,
* recoverability-focused operational design.

### Rationale

The enterprise intentionally avoids separation between:

* architecture,
* implementation,
* operations.

Operational reality is treated as a core architectural concern.

## Serverless and Managed Service Preference

The enterprise strongly favors:

* serverless architecture,
* managed services,
* operational automation,
* low-maintenance infrastructure.

### Rationale

The enterprise operates with:

* limited operational capacity,
* limited cognitive bandwidth,
* centralized ownership.

Operational simplicity is therefore considered strategically important.

# Stakeholder and Organizational Tailoring

## Family-Scale Stakeholder Model

Stakeholders are treated as:

* active enterprise participants,
* operational users,
* socio-technical actors,
* capability influencers.

The enterprise intentionally uses:

* lightweight stakeholder involvement,
* informal feedback loops,
* iterative observation-based evolution.

### Rationale

Formal enterprise stakeholder governance would be disproportionate to enterprise scale.

## Architecture as Learning and Capability Development

The enterprise explicitly treats architecture work as supporting:

* professional development,
* cloud learning,
* certification preparation,
* experimentation,
* recreational and creative goals.

### Rationale

Learning and experimentation are considered legitimate enterprise drivers rather than secondary side effects.

This significantly influences:

* technology choices,
* experimentation tolerance,
* governance flexibility,
* architecture evolution speed.

# Tailoring of Other Architecture Frameworks

The enterprise currently incorporates selected concepts from:

* cloud-native operational practices,
* DevOps culture,
* architecture-as-code approaches,
* event-driven architecture,
* repository-driven governance models.

These practices are applied pragmatically rather than formally.

The enterprise currently does not formally adopt:

* ITIL,
* SAFe,
* Zachman,
* COBIT,
* BPMN-heavy governance approaches,
  although selected ideas may occasionally influence architecture thinking.

# Overall Assessment

The current tailoring approach is considered:

* appropriate for enterprise scale,
* operationally sustainable,
* governance-compatible,
* experimentation-friendly,
* realistic given current organizational constraints.

The enterprise intentionally prioritizes:

* practical usefulness,
* sustainability,
* adaptability,
* learning value,
* operational realism,
  over:
* framework completeness,
* formal process maturity,
* heavyweight governance structures.

The resulting architecture approach can best be described as:

* lightweight enterprise architecture,
* repository-driven governance,
* socio-technical architecture practice,
* cloud-native operational architecture,
* experimentation-oriented incremental evolution.
