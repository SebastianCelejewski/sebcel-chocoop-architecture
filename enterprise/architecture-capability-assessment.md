# Overview

This document describes the current Architecture Capability of the Celejewski family enterprise ecosystem.

Architecture Capability represents the enterprise's ability to consciously design, evolve, govern, document, operate, and maintain architecture across organizational, application, operational, and technological domains.

The ecosystem operates as a small but increasingly mature socio-technical environment combining:

* household coordination
* cloud-native systems
* operational governance
* experimentation
* software engineering practice
* enterprise architecture learning

The current capability level emerged incrementally through practical system evolution, operational experience, migration activities, architectural experimentation, and formal cloud architecture learning, including achievement of the AWS Solutions Architect – Associate certification.

# General Capability Characteristics

The enterprise currently demonstrates strong practical architecture capability despite its small organizational size.

Architecture activities are primarily driven by:

* continuous experimentation
* iterative system evolution
* operational problem-solving
* learning objectives
* practical household needs

Architecture capability is intentionally lightweight and highly integrated with day-to-day development and operational work.

The enterprise strongly favors:

* incremental evolution
* operational safety
* practical validation
* repository-driven governance
* architectural learning through implementation
